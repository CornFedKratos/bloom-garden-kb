The Child

This is built for them.
Everything else is secondary.

The app should feel like putting on a weighted blanket.

What they experience

A magical garden that's always slightly different. A creature companion who is always calm and always glad to see them. Mini-games that feel like pure play. No tasks. No failure. No obligation to return — only curiosity.

What they never experience

A broken streak. A hungry pet. A level they failed. A prompt to keep going. A notification with guilt. A timer. A score. A wrong answer.

🌱

🌊

✨

The Problem

The current care model
breaks down between sessions.

⏳

The wait is long

Average wait for a pediatric OT appointment: 6–12 months. Sessions occur weekly or bi-weekly at best. In between, families are on their own.

🌫️

Parents lack visibility

Between sessions, parents have no clinical guidance, no insight into patterns, and no tool appropriate for their child's sensory and developmental profile.

🔭

OTs are flying blind

OTs have no visibility into how their patients are functioning at home. The home program rarely works — because no one can see it not working in real time.

Too clinical, not engaging

Choiceworks. Zones of Regulation. OTs recommend them. Children don't want to use them independently. Functional tools with no joy.

Engaging, not clinical

Finch. Moshi. Built for neurotypical teens. Obligation mechanics — streaks, pet hunger, daily tasks — are actively contraindicated for this population.

◈ The Gap

No tool currently connects the child, the parent, and the OT in a continuous care system. That gap is what Bloom Garden fills.

◈ The Core Design Decision

The companion is always slightly ahead of the child on the calm curve. It models regulation — it never mirrors distress. It does not need the child. It invites the child. "Come join me" not "fix me." This is the clinical boundary that everything else is built on.

The Mini-Games

Pure play on the surface.
Clinical observation underneath.

Every mini-game passes one test: would a neurotypical child with no therapeutic need just want to play this? The emotional data capture is invisible — a byproduct of genuine play, never the point of it.

🌧️

Rain Painter

Draw rain with your finger. Fast strokes make storms. Slow strokes make mist.

Touch pressure → arousal level

🪨

Stone Stacker

Place river stones one by one. Physics are real but forgiving. How high can you go?

Precision → frustration tolerance

🫧

Bubble World

Bubbles hold tiny surprises. Pop or carry. Blow into the mic to make new ones.

Breath pattern → regulation state

🎵

Mood Orchestra

Touch the garden to make sounds. The companion adds its own harmony to yours.

Tempo → arousal rhythm

◈ The "I Wonder If..." Hook

Rain Painter: "I wonder if I can make a rainbow today." Stone Stacker: "I wonder how high I can go now." Bubble World: "I wonder what's in the bubbles today." Every return is driven by curiosity, not obligation. That's the fundamental difference from every other wellness app in this category.

Daily Rhythm

As routine as brushing teeth.

The garden attaches to the child's existing day. Not through notifications or obligations — through quiet moments that are already there.

🌅

Morning Dew

Something always changed overnight. A new flower. A creature left a gift. A spider built a web. The child returns to see what happened — not to complete a task.

☀️

Weather Report

🌙

The Tuck-In

At the child's bedtime window, fireflies appear. The garden dims. The companion yawns. The child can tuck them in — or just watch. Both are perfect.

🌧️

Calm Mode

Parent taps one button after a meltdown. In two seconds: gentle rain, soft ambient, companion in quiet co-regulation. No sound spike. No demands. Just the garden, waiting.

🌱

No Wrong Day

The garden never withers. The companion is never hungry. A child away for a week returns to a garden that had a quiet time — and something new arrived while things were still.

The Parent

Insight without effort.

The parent dashboard builds itself. Every two-second weather check-in, every mini-game session, every calm mode trigger — all of it accumulates silently into a picture parents have never had before.

Emotional timeline

30 days of weather check-ins as a visual pattern. No numbers. No scores. Just the shape of how their child has been feeling over time.

Activity patterns

Which mini-games does the child gravitate toward during likely dysregulation windows? When does the app get opened? What time of day is hardest?

Weekly summary

A plain-language AI-generated brief — max three sentences. Always followed by: "This is not a clinical assessment. Discuss with your OT." The OT is always the authority.

Context flags

"Had a hard afternoon at school." "New medication started." A free-text field that adds the story behind the data — visible to the linked OT when they prep for a session.

Gentle anomaly alerts

When behavioral patterns change significantly, the parent sees a quiet note: "We noticed a change in [child's] patterns this week that may be worth discussing." No clinical language. No alarm.

The OT Portal — Garden Keepers

Continuous care.
Between sessions.

OTs see 3–5 million pediatric patients with a 6–12 month waitlist to access them. Between sessions, they're flying blind. Bloom Garden changes that.

🌱

Seed Packets

The OT builds a custom exercise bundle and pushes it to the child's garden as a glowing seed. No notification. No demand. The child discovers it naturally when they're ready.

📬

Garden Mail

A short message delivered by a garden creature. Max three sentences. Read aloud. 30 seconds of OT effort. Potentially meaningful to a child who struggles with consistency between sessions.

📊

Passive Observation

Emotional check-in trends. Which activities the child chose during likely dysregulation. Time-of-day patterns. What they avoid. All of it waiting before the session starts.

Session Prep Brief

AI-generated pre-session note from behavioral data. "Since your last session with Maya, here are the patterns worth discussing." The OT still makes every clinical decision — we just save 20 minutes of manual prep.

The Clinical Boundary

Bloom Garden is a home program tool that extends OT care between sessions. It does not provide occupational therapy. It does not replace clinical judgment. The OT is the authority — always.

Clinical Integrity Layer

We built the governance
before the product.

Every clinical or therapeutic claim in Bloom Garden operates under the Clinical Integrity Layer — a formal governance protocol established on day one, before a single line of spec was written.

Phase 1 — Research Gate

Before any feature enters development: is there peer-reviewed evidence for this mechanism in autistic children specifically? Or are we extrapolating? Extrapolations are documented, not hidden.

Phase 2 — Clinical Gate

After prototype: OTs observe the mechanic with real children before approving. Not theoretical sign-off — observation-based. What OTs are trained to do. Not what we can produce in a deck.

Four Evidence Briefs completed

Co-regulation theory. Projective play. Diaphragmatic breathing. Proprioceptive regulation. Each with supporting citations, complicating evidence, documented extrapolations, and OT validation questions.

◈ The Governing Principle

"We help children. We do not seek validation for ourselves."

This sentence is in the MISSION.md. Verbatim. At the top. Every agent reads it at every session.

AI never diagnoses

AI summarizes, patterns, and flags. AI never diagnoses, prescribes, or replaces clinical judgment. Every AI output visible to parents or OTs carries a mandatory disclosure.

Technology & Compliance

HIPAA is the foundation.
Not a feature we'll add later.

Flutter — iOS + Android

Phone and tablet, both platforms from day one. Tablet-optimized layouts — larger touch targets matter enormously for this population. Offline-first architecture for cars, waiting rooms, rural areas.

Supabase + pgvector

Row-level security on all PHI tables. BAA required before any health data enters the system. Edge Functions for all AI processing — PHI never leaves our infrastructure. US data residency enforced.

AI layer — post-MVP

The infrastructure collects clean outcome data from day one. The AI recommendation layer builds on top of real behavioral patterns — not assumptions. We build the foundation first.

HIPAA

PHI encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Audit logging on all PHI access. Breach notification protocol defined pre-launch. Every vendor touching PHI has a signed BAA.

COPPA

Parental consent gate for all child account creation. No child independent credentials. No advertising. No behavioral data sharing. Data deletion on parent request, complete and verifiable.
