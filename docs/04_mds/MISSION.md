# Bloom Garden — Mission & Governing Principles
**Status:** Constitutional — requires Orchestrator approval to modify
**Date Established:** 2026-03-22

---

## Mission
Bloom Garden exists to support autistic children in developing emotional regulation skills through joyful, non-coercive play — connecting them with their parents and occupational therapists as a continuous care system between clinical sessions.

---

## Who We Serve
The child first. Always.
The parent second.
The OT third.
No feature ships that inverts this order.

---

## What We Are
- A HIPAA-compliant pediatric emotional regulation support platform
- A home program tool that extends OT care between sessions
- A parent insight tool that builds understanding without burden
- A clinical resource that grows more valuable with every child who uses it

---

## What We Are Not
- A therapy replacement
- A diagnostic tool
- A behavioral compliance system
- An engagement-maximizing consumer app
- A social platform
- A data product

---

## Non-Negotiables
These cannot be overridden by product decisions, timeline pressure, scope negotiation, or agent output.

1. Every clinical claim requires evidence before it enters spec
2. HIPAA compliance is architectural, not additive
3. No feature places emotional burden on the child
4. No failure states, ever
5. The child's data is sacred
6. No ads, ever
7. AI summarizes, patterns, and flags — AI never diagnoses, prescribes, or replaces clinical judgment
8. OT sign-off on clinical mechanics requires prototype observation, not theoretical review
9. The OT portal is not optional — it is core infrastructure
10. COPPA compliance is absolute

---

## The Principle That Governs Everything

*We help children. We do not seek validation for ourselves.*

---

## For Every Agent on This Project
Before you build anything, ask:
- Does this serve the child first?
- Is there clinical evidence for the mechanism this implements?
- Does this place any burden, obligation, or failure state on the child?
- Is PHI handled correctly in this implementation?
- Would an OT recommend this to a family?

If you cannot answer yes to all five — stop and flag it before proceeding.
