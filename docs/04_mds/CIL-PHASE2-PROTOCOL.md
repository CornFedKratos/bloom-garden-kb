## Purpose

This document governs the CIL Phase 2 clinical gate. Phase 2 sign-off requires an OT to observe a real child using the prototype and provide a determination per mechanic. No theoretical sign-off is accepted. No proxy observation is accepted. The OT must be present and the child must be real.

This document is provided to the OT before the session. It tells them exactly what we are asking, how to observe, and what their determination means.

---

## What We Are Asking

A single observation session with one child from your existing caseload.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Child profile** | Autistic, ages 4–12, able to engage with a touchscreen independently |
| **Session duration** | Child-led — up to 60 minutes. Session ends when the child disengages. Do not prompt the child to continue. |
| **OT role** | Observe and note. Do not direct the child's interaction. Do not demonstrate the app. Do not coach. |
| **Setting** | Wherever the child is most regulated — home, clinic, or therapy room. Your call. |
| **Materials** | The prototype app on an iOS device (provided). This observation form (printed or digital). |
| **Audio** | The prototype includes ambient sound and interaction audio (meadow wind, rain, stone placement, companion responses, co-regulating breathing drone). Sound is at 40% max system volume with no sudden onsets. **Device volume should be set to a comfortable level before the session begins.** The OT is evaluating the full audiovisual experience — not visual-only. If for any reason the session must be conducted without sound, note this on the observation form as it is a significant clinical variable. |

## Before The Session

**OT preparation (approximately 30 minutes):**
1. Read the Bloom Garden MISSION.md — understanding the governing principle matters for the observation
2. Read the Clinical Integrity Layer document — understand the two-phase governance model
3. Read the four Clinical Evidence Briefs (CEB-001 through CEB-004) — the mechanics you're observing have a documented evidence base, and knowing it will sharpen your observations
4. Read PROTOTYPE-SCOPE.md — know exactly what is in the prototype and what you're evaluating

**Child and parent preparation:**
1. Parent provides informed consent for the observation session
2. Child does not need to be briefed — the prototype is designed to be approached without instruction
3. Ensure the child is in a regulated or approaching-regulated state before the session begins. Do not begin the session during or immediately after a dysregulation event.

---

## During The Session

**Your role is observer, not facilitator.**

- Place the device in front of the child. Do not demonstrate.
- Do not say "try this" or "what do you think?" or "do you want to stop?"
- If the child asks you what to do, you may say "whatever you'd like."
- Observe using the framework below. Brief notes are sufficient — paper is fine.
- Session ends when the child puts down the device, moves away, or clearly disengages. That is data, not failure.

**If the child shows distress at any point:** Follow your standard clinical judgment. The session can end at any time. Note what triggered the distress — this is critical information.

## After The Session

**Same day or next day:**
1. Complete any notes from the session while memory is fresh
2. Schedule a 30-minute debrief with Don (contact below)
3. Submit your completed observation form to Don

**At the debrief:**
- Don and OT review each determination together
- For any Revise determination: specific revision is documented and agreed upon
- For any Remove determination: escalates to Claud3 and Don for PRD impact assessment before any Phase 3 work
- OT signs off on the final determination record

---

## What Constitutes CIL Phase 2 Sign-Off

**All four mechanics must reach Approved status.**

Sign-off is granted when:
- The observation session has occurred with a real child
- The OT has submitted completed observation notes
- The debrief has occurred
- All four mechanics are marked Approved (or Revise iterations are complete and re-observed)
- Don has documented the sign-off in this file and in the Linear RISK-003 ticket

**Sign-off is NOT granted by:**
- Theoretical approval ("I think it would work")
- Approval based on the OT using the prototype themselves
- Proxy observation (watching a video of another child)
- Partial observation (only some mechanics observed)
